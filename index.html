What is the compressibility?
The compressibility is a measurement of the instantaneous relative volume change of a fluid or solid as a response to pressure change.
How to calculate compressibility?
To calculate compressibility you should use the following formula:
β = -(1/v) * (σv/σp)
where:
β = compressibility
v = volume
p = pressure
Why use compressibility?
Compressibility is used in order to measure the ability of something to reduce it's size or volume by application of pressure.
